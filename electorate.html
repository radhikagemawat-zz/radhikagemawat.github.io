ABOUT

Campus elections pertaining to Student Affair Council (SAC) are conducted once every year. Eighty-five candidates are elected for the Council and campaigning by the candidates and their party is an integral part of these elections. However, often the entire process results in unnecessary fiasco and expenses. The extremely short period of campaigning doesn't suffice the need to reach out to all the students who have queries pertaining to same.


SOLUTION

The app, “Electorate” developed by the official technical group of college IMG (Information Management Group) primarily aims to induce transparency in the system. Manifestos shall be uploaded by the candidates, which will be followed by a question-answer session. Questions can be asked from the candidates, and if incase unanswered, they shall be shown as “unanswered”. Each candidate shall have his own profile page, wherein he can access all the questions, answer them, and upload their manifestos. Once the manifesto has been uploaded, it can’t be altered or edited.


PERSONAS

  • PRIMARY

    STUDENTS OF CAMPUS

    • Quick access to all the manifestos of candidates at centre and bhawan level provided, hence an easy and convinient method to compare the authenticity and legibility of manifestos
    • Questions/queries can be posted (only from candidates at centre level and respective bhawans), and shall be answered by the candidate
    • Queries answered will be visible to everyone
    • If queries are left unanswered, would be publicly visible. Hence, transparency of system is enhanced
    • Identity of the person asking questions shall be public; to avoid spamming

  • SECONDARY

    • Intranet platform (accessed by students all across the campus) to display their manifestos during the campaign period
    • Facilitating the process of campaign by providing a medium to answer the queries of students all across, thus able to reach out to relatively a larger sect at a faster pace
    • Visibility of other candidates’ manifestos and their queries section leading to system transparency
    • Unnecessary expense of printing and displaying the manifestos can be avoided
    • Consistent design of manifestos bring uniformity in the system of campaign


GOALS

  • Inducing transparency to the system;
  • Easier then physical campaigning;
  • Bandwidth of interacting with voters substantially increased;
  • Cost effective;
  • Manual labour reduced in terms of campaigning and writing manifestos;
  • Saves time (Question/Answer sessions, uploading/spreading manifestos)
