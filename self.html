Zeta App Screen

Re-Design Exercise

ABOUT

Just for exploring new dimensions, I decided to re-design the apps screen for Zeta. Current design scheme of the apps' page had the all the usable apps in one screen, without categorising their purpose. Moreover, the listing has been done without any alphabetical order. In order to make the view concise and user-friendly, the following design plan was executed.


ITERATION-1

  • Apps were categorised into: mobile, shopping, food&entertainment, and health&care

ITERATION-2

  • Categorisation of apps
  • Size of icons reduced to 20px
  • Navigation for multiple apps in the same category
  • Bottom border for each category


ITERATION-3

  • border shifted near the title to highlight the title more significantly

ITERATION-4

  • the border was removed to make the interface look smooth and seamless

ABOUT

I personally felt that I needed an app on the lines of travel, which maintained all my records of the places visited, places I wish to visit and attach memories to those. Then I came up with a concept on lines of "Travelogue": A log of my travel activities. Further, I decided to have a very minimal interface, limiting to only self use; i.e. no social interactions around. But for that very reason, incentive had to be provided since users prefer to share their activities with others.
However, for me my personal accounts need to personal and only within my reach. So for that very reason I decided to create a concept on lines of travel-log.
